What do Agility means

Agility is not a process, it’s the ability to adapt to our complex and fast paced environment.

What values is it based on ?

  • Working in short iterative cycles rather than widely spaced deadlines, it allows to quickly experiment and verify hypothesis.
  • Trusting in the human capabilities to collaborate and learn together, adapt and be discipled.

Why Swisscom needs more agility

Agility is more like a sprint relay than a marathon.

How can it help Swisscom be and stay a leader

  • By helping to pursue the company’s digital transition.
  • By getting results faster and more consistent products
  • By improving both team and customer satisfaction while focusing on collaboration

Being Agile

Keep learning and add things to your backpack along the journey

In a practical way:

  • Focus more on customer value and less on the company itself or hierarchy.
  • Interact actively with people. Be transparent yet open to frequent feedbacks and others ideas.
  • Don’t stick to the plan, be open to change and innovation.
  • Formulate, document and experiment hypotheses.
  • Accept uncertainty, which involves to experiment and keep learning along.

Doing Agile

Iterating cycles, not straight lines

Doing agile is not a goal in itself or a specific process.

  • Agility can scale to different levels both in the processes and the structure.
  • The practices and organization style may vary depending on the context and match the inherent challenges.
  • No process nor tool is suitable for every situations, no choice is dogmatic.
  • Value small interdsciplinary and autonomous teams.
  • Results are delivered continuously in increments and ideas based on them.

Myths

Don't let the myths win !

Agility is associated with many prejudices. Often, you hear the following myths:

This is all a hype and goes over. It will never work anyway
Read our answer

That's not true. Agile software development has been established for over 30 years and is constantly evolving. It is today the de-facto standard in IT. As Swisscom is transforming itself into a digital enterprise, agile development methods in complex contexts are also "the way to go" for us. Large IT companies have been using scaled agile teams for a long time. The use of Agile in business and in collaboration with IT is now widely used in the industry and is being leveraged in many places - not just startups.

Anything that is not SAFE is old-fashioned
Read our answer

That's not true. That's why we make the distinction between Being Agile and Doing Agile. Being agile, the agile way of thinking, is important for everyone and essential in our dynamic environment. Doing agile, however, according to which working methods, methods or forms of organization I align myself, but must always be adapted to the situation. It is not about modern or unfashionable, but about successful or not successful in the sense of our strategy.

Error culture does not match our quality standards
Read our answer

That's not true. Agile means Built-In Quality: Developing Agile does not mean delivering bad software. Because you can not scale bad code. On the contrary, the goal is to deliver high-quality, release-ready and thus "finished" increments with each sprint that meet the agreed "definition of done" requirements. The (automated) testing, integration and installation of the software is integrated into the team as a task. This will help identify errors early, before they affect you. Bi-weekly retrospectives at the team level, as well as at the end of each program increment, ensure formal learning and improvements for the next iteration. Based on the feedback loops after each program increment (PI), the hypotheses are validated and appropriate scope adjustments made for the future PI. This allows erroneous assumptions to be detected and corrected more quickly.

It does not need any documentation
Read our answer

That's not true. Also in the agile process model, the essential requirements, e.g. Epics or user stories documented and refined according to the processing and knowledge progress. You will be handed over to the teams with acceptance criteria. To the definition of Done also includes topics such as code documentation and release notes. However, compared to the specifications in a waterfall model, the documentation is less static, refining itself as work progresses.

Agility is haphazard work without a goal. There is no planning or control at all. Responsibilities are unclear, everything is without obligation. Everyone does what he wants and how he likes ("Cherrypicking")
Read our answer

That's not true. What is done in the next increment or sprint (epics, features or stories) is controlled very transparently via the portfolio, program or team backlog. The team has the degree of freedom to define HOW. But it is jointly responsible for its commitment to do everything to deliver the results on time and in the best quality. Planning, goal achievement and delivery results are checked biweekly in sprint planning or sprint review by the teams and presented in the system demo. With Agile, the planning is very transparent and credible overall, as it is intensively validated by the teams.

From a governance perspective, agile teams at the end of each sprint and every program increment provide great visibility into resource allocation and goal achievement, and are managed through portfolio and program-level resource allocation.

It is chaos and anarchy
Read our answer

That's not true. The agile setup also complies with requirements such as data protection, security, architecture and operational readiness. Through DevOps approaches, we ensure this true to the principle of "you build it, you run it" by self-responsible and mixed teams. The agile way of working, if applied correctly, is characterized by a very high degree of discipline and transparency.

Agility needs no leadership. And we can not do that anyway, because our bosses can not handle it
Read our answer

That's not true. In agile contexts there is no less, but other leadership activities. The role and responsibilities of traditional leaders change into Servant Leaders, who develop the corporate culture and create conditions for agile, independent work of mixed teams. They support their employees in personal development, provide orientation and focus more on the content.
